Applied Econometrics (Prof. Edson Severnini) — rated 5 stars:
"Of all the classes I have taken this semester, I would say this is the most practical and my favorite one."

Object Oriented Programming in Java (Prof. Martin Barrett) — rated 4 stars:
"This is the class that gets me up in the morning every Monday and Wednesday, with no exception."
Workload: "23 labs in total. There are three homework, three midterms, and one cumulative final exam."

Measuring Social (Prof. Ari Lightman) — rated 2 stars:
"This class was 12 units, so RIP to my GPA... I was disappointed and quite frankly, embarrassed about my standard that I betrayed."
On poor teamwork and lack of professor support: his answer "went from 'Let me try to talk to them' to 'Look, you'll just have to work with what you have'."

Statistics (Prof. Eli Ben-Michael):
"Eli taught this class so passionately that it was a thoroughly enjoyable experience."

Unstructured Data Analytics (Prof. George Chen):
"if you miss one you can easily miss the rest... probably amazing as a researcher but not so much as a teacher."

On Python coursework workload:
"Each homework felt like Python project that would take days to complete."

Final advice to prospective students:
"Your experience at CMU is what you make out of it... The most important thing is what you learned at the end of the day."
