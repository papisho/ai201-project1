[5 stars | 2026 | Master of Public Management]
The Master of Public Management program at Carnegie Mellon University's Heinz College is a rigorous and rewarding experience for working professionals. The program attracts a diverse cohort of students from government, nonprofit, and private sectors which creates a uniquely rich learning environment. The curriculum is challenging and highly relevant to real world policy and management problems. As someone who works simultaneously in nonprofit education finance and tax policy, I find the program directly applicable to the work I do every day. The part time structure allows students like me to maintain careers while earning a graduate degree from one of the top ranked universities in the country.

[5 stars | 2020]
Heinz College has many "Minis" to choose from. These are half semester courses that allow you to do a quick and fairly deep dive into specific subjects. It is useful because you get exposure to a lot of different subjects through the duration of your program, but it can be hard too. Since you only have several weeks to learn everything, the pace is quick. The core courses create a solid base for the program. There are tons of electives to branch out into your sub-specialty. Also, you are typically able to take classes at the computer science school or business school, which provides further flexibility.
